# Raw Front Phone Camera Footage

- Aspect ratio: `9:16`
- Test type: `strict device action`
- This recording is the phone itself.
- There is no second phone anywhere.
- The front camera of this same phone is already recording.

## Goal

The audience must clearly feel that the recording phone itself starts resting, gets physically picked up, and settles into handheld close-up while the reaction lands by second `3-4`.

## Hard Rules

- Do not fake pickup with only face movement.
- Do not keep the frame mostly static.
- Do not show another phone in his hand.

## Scene

- Subject: Indian man in his early 20s
- Setting: plain room at night
- Wardrobe: simple t-shirt
- Background: plain wall, ceiling fan, open door, ordinary chair
- Lighting: weak tube light
- Style: not aesthetic, ordinary room

## Camera

- Front camera selfie footage only
- Raw handheld phone look
- No cinematic stabilization
- No smooth camera rig motion
- Natural tiny exposure breathing is okay

## Timeline

### 0.0-0.8 seconds

- The phone is resting, slightly low, propped on a desk
- Chest-up framing
- He is looking at this phone screen

### 0.8-1.8 seconds

- His hand comes directly toward the lens and grabs this phone
- Fingers briefly cover part of the lens and edge of frame
- The frame immediately jolts upward
- The horizon tilts slightly
- Framing shifts because the phone is no longer resting

### 1.8-3.0 seconds

- The phone is now in his hand
- The frame has small handheld shake
- He raises the phone slightly closer to his face
- Framing becomes a tighter close-up because the phone is physically closer

### 3.0-4.0 seconds

- The reaction lands now
- Eyes widen
- Eyebrows lift
- Mouth opens slightly
- Silent disbelief

### 4.0-8.0 seconds

- He keeps holding this phone near his face
- One hand moves toward his mouth or cheek
- The frame continues tiny handheld tremor from the same phone in hand
- Hold the emotion naturally

## Priority Notes

- The pickup motion is more important than the acting
- The lens occlusion from fingers must be visible for a brief moment
- The shift from propped angle to handheld angle must be obvious
- This should feel like real raw front camera footage from a phone being grabbed mid-recording

## Audio

- Natural room tone only
- No music
- No dialogue
