High-level summary from the March 8, 2026 export:
- `629` responses
- session rating skew is positive: `386` rated `5`, `108` rated `4`, `55` rated `3`, `50` rated `1`, `30` rated `2`
- top use case is `to learn` (`358`), then `to do homework` (`118`), then `to prep for a test` (`57`)

What users value:
- clear explanations
- step-by-step breakdowns
- feeling like they actually understand, not just get an answer
- depth and follow-up clarification

Main friction themes:
- response speed / waiting
- voice issues or speech pace
- whiteboard / notes usability
- wanting text, subtitles, or readable on-screen support
- occasional glitches / cutoffs / hearing issues

Content implications:
- strongest creative angle is `it actually explains the answer`
- second strongest angle is `I finally understood it`
- avoid ads that over-index on flashy AI hype without showing comprehension
- reaction formats should pivot into app proof that shows explanation clarity, not just “wow this exists”
- feature-proof content should directly address the friction points: speed, readable steps, whiteboard clarity, subtitles/text support

Suggested reaction hooks to test:
- `Wait... this actually explains the answer step by step 😳`
- `Why did nobody tell me this study app actually helps you understand 😭`
- `Not me finally getting the problem after one explanation 🤯`
- `This feels better than getting just the answer 📚`
- `It literally breaks the whole thing down for you...`

Suggested follow-on demo/message angles:
- `step-by-step, not answer-only`
- `homework help when you are stuck`
- `learn it fast before the test`
- `clearer voice + readable explanation on screen`
- `whiteboard / notes / visual explanation proof`
