# Insights From User Feedback

## Core product truth
Users are not mainly praising novelty. They are praising `understanding`.

The strongest repeated positive signal is some version of:
- `it explained it clearly`
- `it broke it down step by step`
- `it helped me understand`

That means the best-performing content should not center on generic AI amazement alone. It should center on:
- comprehension
- relief
- clarity
- speed to understanding

## What content to create next

### 1. Reaction -> “I finally get it”
Use the reaction format you’ve already been refining, but the hook should be about comprehension, not app discovery in the abstract.

Best examples:
- `Wait... this actually explains it step by step 😳`
- `I thought this was just another homework app 😭`
- `Why did nobody tell me this app makes it make sense`

Then the demo should immediately show:
- a problem
- the explanation flow
- the answer being broken down

### 2. Pain -> clarity UGC
Create direct-to-camera or interview content around:
- `I knew the answer pattern, but not the concept`
- `I keep getting the answer wrong because nobody explains why`
- `I needed it slower`
- `I needed to both hear it and read it`

This maps directly to what users asked for.

### 3. Whiteboard / notes proof content
Several users mention whiteboard, notes, and wanting to keep or better use notes.

Create content like:
- `POV: the whiteboard finally makes the problem click`
- `When the app writes it out instead of just telling you`
- `This is the only reason I understood the chapter`

### 4. Speed / wait-time objection handling
Users like the explanations, but some complain about waiting, lag, or pace.

So create ad variants that imply:
- quick help
- fast explanation
- immediate clarity

But avoid overstating if the product does not always feel instant.

### 5. Voice + text / subtitle angle
Some users explicitly want text back, subtitles, or both hearing and reading.

This suggests content angles like:
- `I can hear it and read it at the same time`
- `When the explanation is finally readable`
- `For people who need it slower and clearer`

## Best content lanes by priority
1. `reaction + demo` focused on step-by-step understanding
2. `confession / POV` around finally understanding homework
3. `whiteboard proof` content
4. `pre-test rescue` content
5. `voice + text clarity` content

## Good message pillars
- `understand, not just answer`
- `step by step`
- `homework rescue`
- `clear explanation`
- `see it and hear it`
- `less confusion, more clarity`

## Things to avoid in creative
- pure AI hype with no product proof
- vague “study smarter” claims
- overpromising perfection on speed or audio if experience still has friction
- making the app feel only like an answer bot
